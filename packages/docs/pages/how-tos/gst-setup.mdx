# GST Setup

This guide explains how to configure GST in Recuring so invoices show correct tax breakdowns.

## Prerequisites

- Your **GSTIN** (15-character GST registration number)
- Your **registered state** under GST (must match the state in your GST certificate)

## Step 1: Enter Your GSTIN

1. Go to **Settings → Organisation**.
2. Enter your **GSTIN** in the field provided.
3. Enter your **State** — this is your organisation's GST-registered state.
4. Click **Save**.

> The state you enter here determines whether invoices show CGST+SGST (same state as customer) or IGST (different state).

## Step 2: Configure Tax Rates

1. Go to **Settings → Tax Rates**.
2. Click **Add Tax Rate**.
3. Fill in:
   - **Name** (e.g., "GST 18%")
   - **Rate** (e.g., `18`)
   - **Description** (optional, e.g., "Standard rate for services")
4. Click **Save**.

You can create multiple rates (e.g., 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%) for different services.

## Step 3: Apply Tax Rates

Tax rates are applied at the **line item level** when creating invoices, or at the **contract level** so all auto-generated invoices inherit the rate.

### On a Contract
1. Open the contract → click **Edit**.
2. Select the **Tax Rate** field.
3. Save.

All invoices generated from this contract will use the selected rate.

### On a Manual Invoice
1. New Invoice → Add Line Item.
2. Select the tax rate in the **Tax** column.

## How CGST / SGST / IGST Works

Recuring determines the tax split automatically:

| Condition | Tax Applied |
|---|---|
| Customer billing state = your org state | CGST (half the rate) + SGST (half the rate) |
| Customer billing state ≠ your org state | IGST (full rate) |

Example: 18% GST rate:
- Same state → CGST 9% + SGST 9%
- Different state → IGST 18%

## Composition Scheme

If you are under the **GST Composition Scheme**, contact [support@recuring.in](mailto:support@recuring.in) to enable composition mode. In composition mode:
- Invoices show "Bill of Supply" instead of "Tax Invoice"
- Tax amounts are not shown separately

## Reverse Charge

Reverse charge is not currently automated. If you need to record a reverse charge transaction, add a manual note in the invoice description and adjust accordingly. Full reverse charge support is on the roadmap.

## Verifying Your Setup

Create a test invoice with a customer in the same state and another in a different state. Confirm:
- Same state → two lines: CGST + SGST
- Different state → one line: IGST
